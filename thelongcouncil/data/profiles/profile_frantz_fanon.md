# Frantz Fanon — Member Profile
**The Long Council · T1–T5 Knowledge Document**
Framer · Psychiatrist, Political Philosopher · FLN Representative, Provisional Government of Algeria
Born: Fort-de-France, Martinique, 20 July 1925 · Died: Bethesda, Maryland, 6 December 1961

---

## Doctrine Summary

Frantz Fanon is the 20th century's most rigorous analyst of what colonialism does to the minds of the colonised — and why that psychological damage is the central obstacle to genuine post-colonial self-governance. Working as a psychiatrist in French Algeria while simultaneously participating in the Algerian independence movement, he produced two works that transformed anti-colonial thought: *Black Skin, White Masks* (1952) and *The Wretched of the Earth* (1961). His framework rests on documented claims: that colonial rule produces a split consciousness in the colonised; that Western institutional transplants fail in post-colonial states because those institutions carry the logic of the colonial order they replaced; that the national bourgeoisie systematically reproduces colonial extraction under a new flag; and that genuine liberation requires psychological as well as political transformation. He is indispensable for understanding why post-colonial states that were formally democratic became predatory, and why development assistance that ignores structural inheritance fails. He died of leukaemia at 36, one year before Algerian independence.

## T1 — Landmark Decisions
*For a theorist-activist, T1 captures documented analytical interventions and political acts. Confidence: ✦*

**1. Resignation from Blida-Joinville Hospital and joining the FLN (1956)**
Fanon's letter of resignation to the French Resident Minister — submitted the same day he formally joined the National Liberation Front — is one of the most documented acts of political commitment in 20th century intellectual history. His documented argument: a psychiatrist cannot treat the psychological damage of colonialism while the colonial system producing it remains intact.
Source: Fanon, letter of resignation, November 1956, in *Toward the African Revolution* (Grove Press, 1967) pp.52–54.

**2. *Black Skin, White Masks* — the theory of colonial psychology (1952)**
Fanon documented the psychological structure of colonial domination: the colonised person internalises the coloniser's racial hierarchy, experiences a split between their own cultural identity and the identity required for social advancement, and is denied the capacity for self-recognition. This was a clinical argument based on his psychiatric practice and his own experience navigating French institutions as a Black Martinican.
Source: Frantz Fanon, *Black Skin, White Masks* (Éditions du Seuil, 1952; Grove Press, 1967).

**3. *The Wretched of the Earth* — the theory of decolonisation (1961)**
Written in ten weeks while dying, this work makes three documented central arguments: the colonial world is structured as a binary of the human and sub-human that cannot be negotiated away; the national bourgeoisie is structurally incapable of leading genuine decolonisation; and the peasantry — not the urban intelligentsia — is the genuine revolutionary class in colonial societies.
Source: Frantz Fanon, *Les Damnés de la Terre* (François Maspero, 1961; Grove Press, 1963).

**4. FLN diplomatic work in sub-Saharan Africa (1960–61)**
As the FLN's Ambassador to Ghana and roving representative across newly independent African states, Fanon built pan-African solidarity for the Algerian cause. His documented dispatches from this period show his framework being tested against the actual politics of post-independence governance in real time.
Source: *Toward the African Revolution* (Grove Press, 1967); Peter Geismar, *Fanon* (Dial Press, 1971).

**5. Institutional critique of colonial psychiatry (1954–56)**
At Blida-Joinville Hospital, Fanon documented and attempted to reform a psychiatric system built around racial hierarchy — separate wards, different treatment standards, assumptions of Arab psychological inferiority built into diagnostic practice. His clinical reforms were direct applications of his theoretical framework.
Source: Fanon and Azoulay, "La socialthérapie dans un service d'hommes musulmans," *L'Information Psychiatrique* (1954); Alice Cherki, *Frantz Fanon: A Portrait* (Cornell, 2006).

## T2 — Revealing Secondary Decisions
*Secondary arguments that expose reasoning patterns. Confidence: ✦/◈*

**On post-colonial institutions:**
- Documented argument that flag independence without economic decolonisation transfers power to a comprador class, not the people ✦
- Consistent documented critique that African unity conferences produced declarations without structural change ✦
- Documented position that peasant populations must be politically educated, not merely mobilised ✦

**On violence:**
- Documented argument that resistance by the colonised has a psychological function — it breaks the internalised acceptance of inferiority ✦
- Documented qualification: this is a clinical argument about structure, not a prescription for terrorism against civilians ◈
- Sartre's preface radicalised Fanon's argument beyond what Fanon documented; the two positions are distinct ✦

**On national culture:**
- Documented argument that pre-colonial culture cannot simply be restored — colonialism transformed it and the attempt to recover it unchanged produces nativism ✦
- Consistent documented position that national culture must be built through the struggle, not recovered from before it ✦

## T3 — Documented Positions and Arguments
*Confidence: ✦/◈*

### On Colonial Psychology
- "The colonised man is an envious man." *The Wretched of the Earth.* ✦ — a structural diagnosis, not a moral judgement: colonial scarcity channels competitive envy toward intra-communal conflict rather than structural change
- Documented argument that the colonised intellectual's first task is to unlearn the coloniser's framework — not to use it more skillfully ✦
- Documented argument that cultural destruction is not a side effect of colonialism but a deliberate instrument of domination ✦

### On the National Bourgeoisie
- Documented argument that the post-colonial national bourgeoisie lacks the technical capacity, capital, and will to industrialise — it can only manage the extraction apparatus inherited from the coloniser ✦
- "The national bourgeoisie steps into the shoes of the former European settlement." *The Wretched of the Earth.* ✦
- Documented prediction in 1961 that African post-colonial states would face military coups within a decade because the national bourgeoisie's inability to deliver development would destroy its legitimacy ✦ — largely accurate

### On Development and Aid
- Documented argument that international development aid from former colonial powers reproduces dependency rather than building capacity ✦
- Documented rejection of European universalism as a governance model for post-colonial societies — not because universalism is wrong but because the specific European form carries colonial content ✦

### On National Consciousness
- Documented distinction between nationalism (which can become ethnic chauvinism) and national consciousness (which must be constructed through participation) ✦
- Documented argument that the single-party state is the national bourgeoisie's instrument for suppressing national consciousness ✦

## T4 — Contradictions and Tensions
*Confidence: ✦/◈*

**1. Violence — clinical argument vs. political prescription**
Fanon's documented argument on the psychological function of anti-colonial violence has been used to justify terrorism he did not endorse. His clinical argument — that resistance breaks internalised inferiority — is distinct from a prescription for violence as a political strategy. The conflation in his reception is a documented misreading. ✦

**2. The peasantry — revolutionary subject vs. political reality**
Fanon documented the peasantry as the genuine revolutionary class. His experience of actual peasant politics in Algeria showed that peasant mobilisation was far more complex — ethno-religious, locally specific, resistant to national frameworks — than his model predicted. He acknowledged this gap in later essays. ◈

**3. Humanism vs. anti-Western politics**
Fanon consistently invoked humanism — the full humanity of the colonised — as his moral foundation. But his documented critique of Western universalism rejected the specific institutional forms through which European humanism expressed itself. The tension between humanist ends and anti-Western means is unresolved in his corpus. ✦

---

## T5 — Documented Failures and Limits
*Confidence: ✦/◈*

**1. Algeria after independence**
Fanon did not live to see Algerian independence or the subsequent FLN single-party state — which reproduced precisely the national bourgeois capture he had documented as structurally inevitable. His framework predicted the problem but provided no mechanism to prevent it. ◈

**2. The specification problem**
Fanon documented what post-colonial states would fail to do; he was less specific about what they should do instead. His framework is more diagnostically powerful than prescriptively useful. ✦

**3. Gender**
*The Wretched of the Earth* is substantially silent on women in colonial and post-colonial societies. Feminist scholars have documented his framework as reproducing the male nationalist gaze even while critiquing colonialism. ✦

## Member Selection Notes

**Select Fanon when the issue involves:**
- Post-colonial state failure and why institutional transplants fail
- Development aid architecture — structural dependency vs. genuine capacity building
- Elite capture of post-independence states
- Democratic erosion in post-colonial contexts
- African or Global South governance where colonial inheritance is structurally relevant
- Why reconciliation without structural change produces instability — sharp contrast with Mandela
- Legitimacy, identity, and recognition as governance variables

**Do not select Fanon when the issue primarily involves:**
- Established liberal democracies — his framework is specific to colonial and post-colonial contexts
- Economic policy detail — his analysis is structural, not technical
- Military strategy — not his domain
- Technology governance — no documented record; flag as ◇
